Milestone 2 - The Most Famous People You've Never Heard of¶

By Sarah Helmbrecht and JT McDermott

https://skhelmbrecht.github.io/

I. Introduction¶

In our modern cultural ecosystem, famous people and celebrities dominate, and in the age of the internet and social media it has never been easier to become famous and gain a global audience. However, their still exists great cultural stratificaiton across both geography, age, and other lifestyle prefences. This inspired the question: who are the most famous people that we don't know exist?

An Example¶

If you don't speak Hindi, you probably have no idea who the gentleman in the picture below is. Yet, Akshay Kumar is one of the highest paid actors in the world. According to Forbes, Kumar earned over $48.5 million in 2020. Kumar is one of the most famous and successful actors in Indian cinema, or Bollywood, which is significant because India is the second largest country on Earth with close to 1.4 billion people.

Context¶

To understand our approach to answering this question, it helps to understand the perspective of us, the authors. We are both Gen-Z college students that were raised in the United States and primarily speak English.

The main factors that might hide an otherwise famous person from us are language, geography, time, and our own personal preferences. Language and geography are simple to understand; people are generally unlikely to be exposed to media in a language they don't speak from a foreign country. This is increasingly changing with the advent of the international stars

Motivation¶

The initial motivation for this study was pure curiosity, stemming from a desire to better understand the world and the people who live in it. There are practical motivations as well, namely:

  1. Identifying potential partners for advertisers
  2. Understanding global trends in media and entertainment

Celebrity endorsements continue to be a major tool for advertisers, and can dramatically improve the ethos of a company when trying to penetrate a new market. Knowledge of famous people and particularly where and why they are famous is important for a successful partnership. For creators and producers of media, understanding trends and expanding horizons invites new influences, new ideas, and ultimately a better end product.

II. Methodology¶

Approach¶

In our study, we will focus on people famous from the world of entertainment, mainly music, film, and television. We will identify persons of interest by analyzing international and historical data that indicate fame, searching for stand out individuals that fall outside of our target demographic.

Proxies for Fame¶

The main issue we must grapple with when trying to answer this question is that fame is a nebulous concept. People don't just walk around with a fame score above their heads, so instead we must employ certain proxies to determine how famous a person is. These proxies include

  • Film box office
  • TV Ratings
  • Music streams and streaming rankings
  • Album/single sales
  • Web searches
  • Social media followers
  • Polls

Assessing the volume of these factors over space and time will bring otherwise unknown-to-us people into our attention.

IV. Plausible Models¶

We plan to test the correlation between a musical artist's overall fame and their fame among millenials.

Two other potential models: One possible model based on our analysis so far would be a predictor of whether or not a person of a certain demographic background will know/be familiar with a celebrity given information about the celebrity's background like nationality, primary language, most recent work, and age.

Another possible model we could consider based on this premise is a quantitative estimator of potential reach of a celebrity, in total potential likely fans, given the celebrity's demographics, language, and media type.
